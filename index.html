PhoneBuzz
Web App | November 2017

Info

As a part of a technical challenge for LendUp, I was tasked with creating a web app that lets users "play" the game FizzBuzz through a phone call.

I built the app using Flask, the Twilio API, and SQLite. Users can either call my Twilio number or request a call to their phone (either instantly or after a delay). Once the call is initiated, the user enters a number through their keypad and hears the "Fizz Buzz" sequence from 1 to that number. All calls/numbers are stored in an SQLite database and the history is shown in a table. Users can replay any call if they wish.

Bipartisan
Web App | February 2017

With the advent of "fake news" recently and the growth of increasingly biased media articles, my team and I set out to build something that would help citizens sift through the political landscape by getting only the most truthful news.

Bipartisan uses machine learning and natural language processing to identify the most credible articles by computing and assigning a 0-100 credibility rating. Users can also view a heatmap of the world and see the top news (arranged by topic) and most credible articles for those topics in any given location.
